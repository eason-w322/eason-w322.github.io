Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

In the close‑up photo on the left, my friend is holding the phone very close to my face. The wide‑angle lens exaggerates my features. On the right, they stood farther back and zoomed in, giving a more natural and flattering look.

Part 2

Architectural Perspective Compression

The wide-angle view makes the lamp appear isolated and tall. With a longer lens, the space compresses, bringing background elements visually closer to the subject.

Part 3

Dolly Zoom

As the camera moves back while zooming in, the background stretches dramatically. This dolly zoom keeps the subject the same size but shifts the surrounding space for a surreal effect.
