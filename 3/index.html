Project 3: Image Warping and Mosaicing

Eason Wei | CS 180 / 280A – Fall 2025

Overview

In this project, I explore image warping and mosaicing to create seamless panoramas from multiple photographs. The goal is to recover homographies between images, warp them using nearest-neighbor and bilinear interpolation, and blend them into smooth mosaics.

  • A.1 – Shoot and Digitize Pictures
  • A.2 – Recover Homographies
  • A.3 – Warp the Images
  • A.4 – Blend into a Mosaic

A.1 – Shoot and Digitize Pictures

I captured multiple photo sets by rotating the camera about a fixed center of projection to create projective transformations suitable for mosaicing. Each image overlaps the next by about 50–60% to ensure reliable feature matching and homography estimation.

Set 1 – Projective Rotation (Panorama)

The sequence above illustrates a panorama obtained by rotating the camera horizontally about its optical center. The images show consistent perspective and lighting, which helps in computing a smooth homography and blending in later steps.
